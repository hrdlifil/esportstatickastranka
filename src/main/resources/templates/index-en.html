OP TAK Grant Project

Digital Tools for Health Support in Esports

We are developing a platform that combines AI, biosensors, and game data to provide players and coaches with measurable support for health, performance, and long-term sustainability.

Why this project

Esports has elite-level demands, but lacks elite-level health support

Identified Problem

  • Growing mental and physical load on players without systematic health monitoring.
  • Fragmented data (sleep, HRV, game metrics) without unified interpretation.
  • Higher risk of overload, sleep issues, eye fatigue, performance drops, and burnout.

Target Change

  • Implementation of data-driven care for players and teams.
  • Risk prediction and real-time training personalization.
  • Bringing esports care closer to standards used in traditional elite sport.

What we are building

Esport Health AI: from signals to recommendations

Data Collection

EEG, HR/HRV, sleep, eye-tracking, physical activity, gameplay telemetry, reaction tests, and daily logs.

AI Analytics

Multimodal data fusion, state estimation, performance forecasting, and fatigue/overload detection.

Personalization

Match readiness, tailored training intensity/type, and individualized recovery protocols.

Practical Interface

Mobile app for players, web panel for coaches, and telemedicine support for specialists.

Experimental design

How the experiments will be conducted

Two-round data collection (target: 500 players)

Round 1: observational data collection, 1 month per player.

Round 2: follow-up AI interventions and recommendation validation.

Pilot verification of methodology takes place in March; full data collection starts on April 1.

Participant Protocol (30 days)

  1. Day 0: informed consent, baseline questionnaires, sensor calibration.
  2. Days 1-30: regular training/play with sensors and synchronized data streams.
  3. Daily log: fatigue, stress, mood, and behavioral factors (sleep, stimulants, unusual events).

1. Dataset preparation

Modality synchronization, artifact cleaning, missing data handling, and feature engineering.

2. Model training

Baseline models for tabular data, sequence models for time series, and multimodal fusion.

3. Prediction and alerts

Stress, fatigue, readiness, and risk of performance decline or overload.

4. Personalized guidance

Dynamic training scheduling, controlled breaks, sleep optimization, and recovery interventions.

Transparency

Frequently asked questions

How is participant data protection handled?

The project includes data anonymization, access-right management, and GDPR compliance throughout the entire data pipeline.

Are the experiments invasive?

No. Measurements are based on non-invasive wearables and standard digital protocols.

Why is the target sample size so large?

A larger data base improves model robustness, supports better generalization, and enables safer personalization.
